Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the universe and the first planet that is the closest to the Sun. Mercury's surface is composed of 5 elements which are sodium, sulfur, potassium, chlorine and thorium. It is a rocky planet that is rich of iron which makes up about 70% of Mercury's total weight. It only takes 88 days for Mercury to revolve around the Sun at an average speed of 105,947 miles (170,505 kilometers) per hour, and is considered to be the fastest among the other planet in the Solar system.

Venus

Venus is the second planet of our Solar system which makes it the second planet closest to the Sun. Venus is known for being the hottest planet in the Solar system, composing of more than 96% carbon dioxide and 3% nitrogen, with small amount of other gases although it composed of mostly gases, Venus is a rocky planet that is made up of inner iron core and rocky mantle. It only takes 225 days for Venus to revolve around the Sun at an average speed of 78,341 miles (126,077 kilometers) per hour.

Earth

Earth is the third planet in the Solar system and is the only planet that is habitable, Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system. Earth is composed of a variety of elements. The Earth's crust has four main component, which are materials, rocks, soil and water that makes life on Earth possible. It takes 365 days for Earth to revolve around the Sun and 366 days during leap year that occurs every 4 years, with the average speed of 67,000 miles (107,000 kilometers) per hour.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet in the Solar system and is known as the red planet, although it is bright as red, Mars is not considered as the hottest planet in the solar system. Mars is a rocky, dry planet and has a thin atmosphere made up mostly of carbon dioxide, argon, nitrogen and a small amounty of oxygen and water vapor. It also has a plenty mineral resources including iron, nickel, titanium, aluminum, sulfur, chlorine and calcium. It takes 687 days for Mars to revolve around the Sun at an average speed of 540,593 miles (870m kilometers) per hour.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth and the largest planet in the Solar system more than twice as massive as all other planets combined. Jupiter's stripes and swirls are actually cold. It has a composition of hydrogen and helium whichk makes it a gas planet. Deep in the atmosphere, pressure and temperature increase, compressing the hydrogen gas into a liquid. This gives Jupiter the largest ocean in the solar system an ocean made of hydrogen instead of water. It takes about 4,333 days for Jupiter to revolve around the Sun at an average seped of 28,273 miles (43,000 kilometers) per hour.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet and the second largest planet next to Jupiter in the Solar system, it has a massive ring of comets, asteroids and icy particles. It is composed of hydrogen and helium like Jupiter which also makes it a gas planet and takes about 10,756 days to revolve around the Sun with the speed of 21,637 miles (34,821 kilometers) per hour.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh or second to the last planet in the Solar system, it is one of two ice giants in our solar system, Uranus is a cold planet and has a strong element that differs from Jupiter and Saturn like carbon, nitrogen, sulfur and oxygen, mixed with an atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. Uranus's core is rocky and its mantle is made up of icy water, methane and ammonia. Uranus is considered the coldest planet that holds the record of -224Â°c ever measured in the Solar system. It takes about 30,687 days for Uranus to revolve around the Sun with the speed of 15,290 (24,607 kilometers) per hour.

Neptune

Neptune is the eight or the last planet in the Solar system, that is composed of mostly hydrogen and helium with a little bit of methane. Neptune is a brighter blue color planet and one of the two icy giant planets, most of the planet's mass is made up of a hot dense fluid of icy materials which are water, methane and ammonia. Neptune is the densest planet among the giant planets. It takes about 60,190 days for Neptune to revolve around the Sun at an average speed of 12,146 miles per hour (5.43 kilometers) per second.
