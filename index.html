NOTABLE MOON WALKERS

Neil Alden Armstrong (1930-2012)

The first man to walk on the Moon. Boarded on the Apollo 11, Gemini 8 space mission as the Commanding officer. And on July 20, 1969, it was then he landed on the surface of the moon, ...claiming it as the first step of a historical event. Prior to embarking his career as an Astronaut, he was previously an engineer, naval aviator, test pilot. He later pursued his master's degree in Aerospace engineering and took his profession as a university professor in Cincinnati, Ohio.

Awards and Honors: Presidential Medal of Freedom, Collier Trophy, Congressional Space Medal of Honor, Goddard Memorial Trophy.

Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin (1930)

He was the second person to step foot on the moon. An american astronaut who boarded the same ship as Neil A. Armstrong, NASA's Apollo 11, Gemini 8 mission. He was a distinguishable figure in the U.S. ... Air Force, whereto he participated in the Korean War. He received his Ph.D. in Austronautics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Awards and Honors: Presidential Medal for Freedom, Collier Trophy, Goddard Memorial Trophy, the Harmon International Trophy, Air Medal, NASA Distinguished Service Medal, Distinguished Service Medal, Hubbard Medal, NASA Space Flight Medal, Distinguished Flying Cross.

Structure

Much like the Earth, the Moon also composes similar attributes such as the core, mantle, and crust.

Crust:

The moon's outermost layer, known as the crust, is between 20 and 40 miles (30 and 60 kilometers) thick. It is mostly composed of stony elements such as calcium, magnesium, oxygen, silicon, and aluminum. The lunar surface of the moon is then distinguised into two regions: The Lunar Highlands, which is the lighter areas of the moon. And Maria, The darker patches which were formed through ancient volcanic activity.

Mantle:

The lunar mantle is located several hundred miles below the surface and is located under the crust. And it is composed of iron and silicates, among other denser rocks and likely contains minerals like olivine, pyroxene, and feldspar.
